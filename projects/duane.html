Duane Syndrome Vision Simulator

Duane syndrome is a rare congenital eye-movement disorder — most people, including many clinicians, have never seen the world the way a patient does. This application changes that: it processes a live camera feed in real time to recreate the double vision and peripheral field loss a patient experiences as their gaze shifts, turning a textbook diagnosis into something you can feel.

/01 Why simulate a disorder?

Duane syndrome limits the eye's ability to move outward (and sometimes inward), so the two eyes stop pointing at the same target as gaze shifts laterally. The brain receives two mismatched images — diplopia — and patients compensate with head turns that clinicians often misread.

An accurate simulation is a powerful empathy and training tool: it lets healthcare professionals, educators, and family members experience the asymmetric, gaze-dependent nature of the condition instead of reading about it. Accuracy matters — the distortions had to be grounded in the actual clinical presentation, not a generic blur filter.

/02 The processing pipeline

Every camera frame passes through a real-time transformation stack, tuned to run at interactive frame rates on CPU alone:

01

capture_frame()

Grabs frames from the live camera interface and normalizes them into NumPy arrays for processing.

02

simulate_gaze()

Models the simulated gaze direction, driving how severe the misalignment is — the disorder's signature is that symptoms scale with lateral eye movement.

03

displace_percept()

Pixel-shifting produces the second, displaced image the misaligned eye would deliver; the shift vector tracks the gaze model.

04

blend_diplopia()

Mask-based alpha blending fuses the true and displaced images the way the visual system perceives them — a dominant percept with a translucent ghost.

05

apply_field_loss()

Gaussian-filtered focus masks degrade the peripheral field asymmetrically, recreating the functional vision loss that accompanies the misalignment.

/03 Engineering highlights

performance

Real-time on CPU

The full displacement-blend-mask stack runs per frame at interactive rates using vectorized NumPy operations — no GPU required, so it runs on any clinic laptop.

accuracy

Clinically grounded

Distortion parameters follow the documented presentation of the disorder: gaze-dependent severity, asymmetric onset, and compensatory-head-turn relief.

interface

Interactive controls

A Tkinter GUI exposes gaze direction and severity in real time, letting an instructor demonstrate exactly how symptoms change as the patient looks left or right.

architecture

Clean separation

GUI layer, image-processing engine, and camera interface are fully decoupled — the processing core is reusable for simulating other visual conditions.

/04 Impact

The simulator works as an accessibility-awareness resource: medical educators can project it during instruction, and families of newly diagnosed patients can understand what their child actually sees. It's also a compact demonstration that real-time computer vision doesn't require heavyweight infrastructure — just careful algorithm design.
